Board of Directors

Advisory Board | Founders

Tom Silver

President

Tom is a Harvard sophomore in Mather House. He is concentrating in Computer Science and Mathematics with a secondary in Statistics. He also has interests in education, molecular biology, and politics. As President of the Digital Literacy Project, he is responsible for overseeing and implementing all domestic and international DLP initiatives. He enjoys coding, coffee, and corgis.

Hannah Blumberg

Director of Curriculum

Hannah is a Harvard sophomore in Cabot House. She is concentrating in computer science and, as Director of Curriculum, will be designing lesson plans for DLP. Hannah tutored for several years in her hometown in New York and is now looking forward to working with the future computer scientists of Massachusetts.

Jenny Choi

Director of Community Outreach

Jenny is a Harvard sophomore in Winthrop. She is responsible for contacting middle schools in the Greater Boston Area, technology corporations, and industry experts about potential partnerships. She also oversees all publicity and recruitment for the organization. Jenny is a very active member of Harvard's Institute of Politics and copresident of Athena, a Harvard-based mentorship program for girls.

Colin Diersing

Director of Education

Colin is a sophomore in Cabot planning to study social studies. He spends much of his time at the Institute of Politics, where he is involved in the Harvard Political Review, Civics teaching program, Harvard Public Opinion Project, and education policy group. He is excited help bring computer science programs to Boston middle schools and get students excited about STEM. He likes coffee and things written by Aaron Sorkin.

Matthew Disler

Director of International Outreach

Matthew is in charge of organizing DLP's efforts to provide computer education to communities globally. A sophomore from Richmond, Virginia, he is interested in comparative politics and international relations. He recently worked at the Justice Studies Center of the Americas in Santiago, Chile, where he authored a study on police protest policy. He is also involved in the Harvard Political Review and Satire V.

Stefanie Grossano

Director of International Education

Stefanie is a sophomore at Boston University studying neuroscience with a minor in education. As Director of International Education, she is responsible for designing curriculum for DLP's international initiatives. Her other contributions to DLP focus on methods for closing the gender disparity in STEM. Stefanie has worked at The New Jersey Department of Education and is an active member of the Community Service Center at BU.

Jesse Shulman

Director of Operations

Jesse is a Harvard sophomore in Mather concentrating in social studies. He has interests in philosophy, film, finance, and creative writing. As Director of Operations, Jesse is responsible for overseeing the financial and legal aspects of DLP. In addition to pursuing independent projects in writing and entrepreneurship, Jesse is on the editorial board of the Crimson.

Ved Topkar

Director of Technology

Ved is a Harvard sophomore in Eliot House. He is interested in biology, chemistry, physics, and computer science and hopes to use this to get children excited about technology. He coordinates partnerships with other organizations, designs lesson plans (especially hardware), and ensures that DLP is using the best technologies possible. He is also involved in The Triple Helix, and the Harvard South Asian Association.
