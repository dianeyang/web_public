Board of Directors

Tom Silver

President

Tom is a Harvard sophomore in Mather House. He is concentrating in Mathematics and Computer Science with a secondary in Statistics. He also has interests in education, molecular biology, and politics. As President of the Digital Literacy Project, he is responsible for overseeing and implementing all domestic and international DLP initiatives. He enjoys coding, coffee, and corgis.

Julianna Aucoin

Director of Finance

Julianna is a Harvard sophomore in Winthrop. Her responsibilities as Director of Finance include implementing fundraising campaigns, writing grants, and handling all legal aspects of DLP. She enjoys the comedy of Louis CK and writes for Harvard's news comedy show, On Harvard Time.

Hannah Blumberg

Director of Curriculum

Hannah is a Harvard sophomore in Cabot House. She is concentrating in computer science and, as Director of Curriculum, will be designing lesson plans for DLP. Hannah tutored for several years in her hometown in New York and is now looking forward to working with the future computer scientists of Massachusetts.

Jenny Choi

Director of Community Outreach

Jenny is a Harvard sophomore in Winthrop. As Director of Community Outreach, Jenny is responsible for contacting middle schools in the Greater Boston Area, technology corporations, and industry experts about potential partnerships with DLP. She also oversees all publicity and recruitment for the organization. Jenny is a very active member of Harvard's Institute of Politics and copresident of Athena, a Harvard-based mentorship program for girls.

Colin Diersing

Director of Education

Colin is a Sophomore in Cabot house planning on studying social studies. He spends much of his time at the Institute of Politics, where he is involved in a range of programs including the Harvard Political Review, Civics teaching program, Harvard Public Opinion Project, and education policy group. He is excited to work with the great DLP team to bring high quality computer science programs to Boston middle schools and get students excited about STEM. He likes coffee and things written by Aaron Sorkin.

Matthew Disler

Director of International Outreach

Matthew Disler is DLP's Director of International Outreach, in charge of planning and coordinating the organization's efforts to provide quality computer education to communities around the world. A sophomore from Richmond, Virginia, he is interested in comparative politics and international relations (but he has not yet declared his concentration). Most recently, he worked at the Justice Studies Center of the Americas in Santiago, Chile, where he authored a study on police protest policy in Latin America, the United States, Spain, and the United Kingdom. He is also involved in the Harvard Political Review and Satire V, a Harvard satire publication.

Stefanie Grossano

When Stefanie is not working on DLP, she loves to bake and has been known to do so at all hours of the night. She hails from a small town in New Jersey.
